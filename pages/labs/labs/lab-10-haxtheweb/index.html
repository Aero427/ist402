You may complete this using hax.psu.edu . Reclaim hosting is running several versions behind on HAXcms at the moment.

HAXcms is a highly flexible, organic static site solution. Because this is a new breed of authoring experience, it needs explaining and people to utilize the tool in different contexts to fully realize it's potential.

As HAX and HAXcms are being developed by the university to improve time on task, increase creativity in course design and help students present themselves online, you can complete this through any of the following scenarios.

Scenario: "Online course"

Instructors don't want to learn new technologies that might get in the way of teaching. They want platforms that are simple to use, recognizable, and that will last. They've seen the ages of LMSs and having to port content to and from WordPress / other CMS solutions in the past. As a result, they need reassurance that HAXcms is easy to use and that it'll be an engaging way for them to teach.

For the purposes of this assignment, we'll take "engaging" to mean funny. So make sure there's lots of memes and fun things in what you're about to develop here. There are a few themes that make sense for presenting an online course but your welcome to use which ever theme you'd like.

Possible topics:

  • A fact driven information site assembled about COVID19 that could help others get relevant information to make better decisions about community health
  • One of the CMSs we've covered (or 10 different ones as an overview of each)
  • Anything personal to you, literally . Just as long as you connect with the material that you want to build into a learning resource for someone else.

Video submission

Your video could be about any of the following in the HAX universe. The point is to generate content that is relevant in "selling" this new technology to a stakeholder group. Any of these are possible submissions

  • Multi-CMS - HAX can be leveraged in Drupal, WordPress (with some manual install work), GravCMS, Backdrop and ClassicPress. Show how to install HAX in any of these other platforms and submit a video about your experience involved in that process. If it makes sense, if you couldn't get it working and what was confusing, are all valid and important outcomes.
  • Your Experience - Describe your frustrations in using HAXcms. What made sense immediately? What took some time to figure out? What's something that worked really well? What's something that still doesn't make sense to you? What did you want it to do that it was missing?

What didn't work or what you ran into issues using the platform are critical to our team getting user feedback and improving.
