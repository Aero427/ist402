Scenario: "Online course for helping in a crisis"

Instructors don't want to learn new technologies that might get in the way of teaching. They want platforms that are simple to use, recognizable, and that will last. They've seen the ages of LMSs and having to port content to and from WordPress / other CMS solutions in the past. As a result, they need reassurance that HAXcms is easy to use and that it'll be an engaging way for them to teach.

For the purposes of this assignment, we'll take "engaging" to mean funny. So make sure there's lots of memes and fun things in what you're about to develop here. There are a few themes that make sense for presenting an online course but your welcome to use which ever theme you'd like.

Create aÂ  ~10-15 page online course about anything you want to teach a friend or family member about. It could be one of the topics from our course or something that you know more about than anyone else.

Topics:

  • A fact driven information site assembled about COVID19 that could help others get relevant information to make better decisions about community health. You will be given 5 bonus points for selecting this topic (yes, 50% of the subject as bonus).
    • Select sources that are not just major media sources. You can include some but lean toward research and source articles. CDC, other countries CDC equivalent, vaccine progress, models of impact of social distancing, things like this. Not just "STOCK MARKET DOWN" and "AIRPORT CLOSE" as so much of the news is right now.
    • This can include information that is geographic specific to yourself and your situation (make it personal). If you live in rural Kansas you have very different distancing considerations than inner city Philadelphia, London, or Queens. The more localized the better chance of connecting with people that know and trust you.
    • Tone should be one about this being serious but not scary. We can help people connect with reality more through the personal trust they have in who is assembling the work
    • Video / Pictures / graphics are important, explaining and linking off to where they are from is also important to ensure people can discover on their own. We're all supposed to be on lock down so we have time to invest in back story
    • You can actually impact the lives of people around you with this. Assemble information in a relevant way, present the case, share out on social media / email to friends and family. Working to improve our reality is why we seek an education. It helps give us purpose. Find that purpose this week.
  • Anything personal to you, literally . Just as long as you connect with the material that you want to build into a learning resource for someone else. It will always be better when you connect with it (but consider the implications of doing the COVID one).
